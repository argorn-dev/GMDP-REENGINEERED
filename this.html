ABOUT GMDP

The most comprehensive database of music and performing artists in Ghana. Documenting Musical Diversity. Promoting Sustainable Musical Futures. 

Background:

The Ghana Music Documentation Project is to develop a comprehensive database on Ghanaian music culture that brings together the various data and resource materials of public and private sector stakeholders for policy makers, academics, administrators, artists and artistes, embassies, foreign visitors and all other interested persons. The Ghana Music Documentation Project would serve as a tool that is (a) accessible to all stakeholders, that ( b) enables stakeholders to use it in different ways that best serve their respective interests and that is c) flexible enough to account for the continuous change of the cultural economy.

The overall significance of the Ghana Music Documentation Project will be in the development of a sustainable tool and database that will benefit researchers as much as policy makers, practitioners, communities, and the public at large. As such, it will also be a major contribution to the Digital Humanities more generally, which have, so far, been centered within North America and Western Europe. Our commitment to cultural and musical sustainability is grounded in the firm believe that it is only within a dynamic and diverse cultural landscape at the grass-roots level that a vibrant market-oriented cultural economy can also grow. At the policy level, the project addresses five of the six implementation areas listed in Ghana’s Cultural Policy. Specifically, these are “preservation and conservation of culture,” “development and promotion of culture,” “presentation of culture,” “establishment of appropriate administrative structures,” and finally “establishment of linkages with various sectors of national development” (NCC, 2004, 15-16).

MEET OUR TEAM

PROFESSOR FLORIAN CARL

Team Leader (Overseeing the policy analysis and general coordination)

Professor Florian Carl holds a PhD in Ethnomusicology (2007) from Hanover University of Music, Drama and Media, Germany. His research interests center around popular music, music technology and contemporary media and sound culture in Ghana and beyond. He has extensive experience in academic administration and project management, serving currently as Head of the Department of Music and Dance and having coordinated major international research collaborations in the past. He is currently a member of the Board of Directors of the DAAD-funded SDG Graduate School “Performing Sustainability”, a collaboration between Ghanaian, Nigerian and German partners. He will be responsible for overseeing and conducting the cultural policy analysis and also the general coordination for the project.

DR ALIMATU-SAADIA YUSSIF

Leading the development of the digital infrastructure and database



Dr. (Mrs.) Alimatu - Saadia Yussiff received her PhD in Information Technology from Universiti Teknologi PETRONAS, Malaysia (2016). She is currently a lecturer in the department of Computer Science and Information Technology, University of Cape Coast, Ghana. Her research areas are Web Technologies, human computer interaction, e-learning, software engineering and the role of information technologies in every arena of life. As an information technologist, Alimatu will lead the development of the digital infrastructure system and database.

Our Sponsors

PROFESSOR SIMON MARIWAH

Professor Simon Mariwah is a Development Geographer with research interests in Environment and Development, Health Behaviours, Sanitation, Waste Management, and Resource Management. Holding a PhD in Geography with specialisation in Environment and Development from the University of Cape Coast (2009), Simon is currently a Senior Lecturer at the Department of Geography and Regional Planning, University of Cape Coast, Ghana. He has good experience in academic administration, serving as the Head of Department of Geography and Regional Planning (2015-2017), and has collaborated with both national and international researchers for close to 10 years on a number of funded research projects on understanding how young in Ghana, Malawi and South Africa navigate new digital technologies in relation to healthcare (ESRC/DFID-funded) and how trust is established in the context of medical uncertainties in Ghana and Tanzania (Welcome Trust-funded). As a Development Geographer, Simon will lead the mapping of music economy in Ghana and also contribute to the organisation of stakeholder workshops.

DR. ERIC DEBRAH OTCHERE

Dr. Eric Debrah Otchere holds a PhD in Music Education (2013) from the University of Cape Coast. He is a Fellow of the American Council of Learned Societies (ACLS), a Fellow of Rhodes University (South Africa) and a Presidential Fellow of the African Studies Association (ASA). As a music educator, his primary interests lie in the Psychology of Music Education, Curriculum Design and Implementation, Measurement and Evaluation of Musical Behaviors and Philosophy of Music Education. In addition to these primary areas, his research has focused on Music, Health and Wellbeing, Music Preference(s) and the use of music in everyday life. He is currently a Steering Committee member of the DAAD-funded SDG Graduate School “Performing Sustainability”, a collaboration between Ghanaian, Nigerian and German partners. In this project, he will be responsible for overseeing the survey of the musical economy and also contribute to the organization of stakeholder workshops among others.

OKYERE-DARKO ADDAI

Mr. Addai, Okyere-Darko holds a BSc in Computer Science (2008) from the University of Cape Coast. He is a Technical head in charge of Central and Western Ghana of the Ghana Academic and Research Network (GARNET). He is also a technical member of the West and Central African Research and Educational Network (WACREN). He is a Systems and Network Engineer at the University of Choice (UCC). As an Engineer in data communication and computer networks, his primary interests lie in the design, deployment and integration of networks, Computer & Information Systems for academic and research networks. In addition to these, he couples as a facilitator in Management Information Systems and Computer Networking at the University of Cape Coast. His area of research is focused on the integration of relevant IT tool for effective teaching & learning in higher education. In this project, he will be responsible for the startup, design, deployment and integration of computing platform for the various Databases, computer system also contribute to the organization of stakeholder workshops among others.
